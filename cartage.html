About the Brand

Cartage is an AI-native logistics platform that functions as an autonomous operating system for freight coordination.

My Deliverable
  • Build a system of icons to be utilized throughout their platform's UI
01

The Challenge

The goal was to design a system of icons that makes Cartage's operating system more intuitive while elevating the brand's visual identity to match their product's quality.

Tech is a notoriously opaque industry. For all the talk about algorithms, data, and AI, it can still be a challenge for customers to understand how it all fits together to meet their business needs. This is great for creating mystique and allure, but at the same time, I think it makes it difficult for tech brands to connect their products with users. They still need to be intuitive enough for someone to sit down at an application and understand what they're looking at and how to interact with it, on day one.

Being an AI native platform, Cartage is at the bleeding edge of modern technology. Part of their struggle is in making the connection between their clients' workflow and the capabilities of their AI. Cartage brought me on to build out a visual language for their operating system. Creating icons to break down the barrier between their advanced system and the average shipping and logistics client. All while leveling up the look of the brand to match the quality of their product.

02

The Approach

Soft, glossy textures and monochromatic color palettes give an ethereal quality, while the objects themselves mimic real-world counterparts that people are familiar with.

I looked back at the ways that tech has explained itself in the past. Apple's use of skeuomorphism was pivotal in making the digital landscape accessible for people to adopt on day one. Today, the minimal, flat-design aesthetic that has been popular in design lately isn't effective in communicating complex subjects. I wanted there to be detail in my designs, the way skeuomorphism is, but I wanted to put a modern twist on it. Just making icons look like real-world objects, in the way Apple did in the early 2000s, was not going to demonstrate the modern ingenuity of the company.

I achieved that modern look by finding a balance between flat illustration and photo realism. The objects are simplified slightly to increase legibility and help with scaling across but subtle textures, transparency and reflections are used to add depth. This allows the objects to feel tangible but not something you might find in the physical world. The Monochromatic yet detailed styling also plays into this same effect.

Integrating their AI agent, Wilson's, face into this icon also ties in the fact that all of these tools are part of their AI system. It lets the customer know that Wilson is connected to every part of the process.

03

The Outcome

This project sets Cartage up to expand on their identity to create a UI that is unique and innovative.

The way I interpreted this project helped the brand understand the bigger picture of how to present itself to the world. It gave them perspective on how to think about their brand as a whole.

100% Decrease in customer calls for walkthroughs and tutorials
+60% Increase in customer comprehension
1 Unified visual language across the entire platform UI
